Visualizations

Figure 3: Top 3 Movies per Genre

This graph presents the IMDb ratings for the top three movies in each genre, highlighting the highest-rated films across various categories. It shows that the top-rated movies generally have high IMDb ratings, typically above 8.0, regardless of genre. Certain genres, such as Documentary, stand out with near-perfect ratings, while other genres like Comedy or Horror show slightly more variability.

Figure 1: Genre vs Runtime Range. Short: 90 minutes or less, Medium: 120 minutes or less, Long: 180 minutes or less

This bubble plot visualizes the distribution of movies by genre and runtime range. The size of each bubble represents the number of movies within each combination. Higher concentration of medium-runtime movies across most genres, especially Drama and Comedy. Long-runtime movies are less frequent and primarily seen in genres like War and History, while short-runtime movies have a more uniform but smaller representation across genres. This suggests that medium-runtime films dominate the industry, regardless of genre, while specific genres may lend themselves to longer or shorter runtimes.

Figure 2: Budget vs Revenue (Interactive Plot) -> Slide the bar to adjust max revenue on x-axis.

This scatter plot illustrates the relationship between movie budget and revenue, categorized by genre (color) and IMDb rating (point size). Higher-budget movies usually generate higher revenue, but there are exceptions where some perform poorly (e.g., "The Flash"). Low-budget films tend to cluster at lower revenue levels, though some achieve higher ratings, suggesting that audience reception isn't exclusively tied to budget.

How to use this graph:

  • Hover Over Points: Interactive tooltips provide details about individual movies, including title, genre, budget, revenue, and IMDb rating.
  • Adjust Filters: Use the budget filter to focus on specific subsets of the data.
  • Compare Genres: Look at color distributions.

Figure 4: IMDB Rating adjusted by Budget Range.

Low Budget: 5 Million and less, Medium Budget: 50 Million to 5 Million, High Budget: 200 Million to 50 Million

This multi-box plot shows the distribution of IMDb ratings across movies categorized by budget range. The median IMDb rating remains relatively consistent across all budget categories, suggesting that budget size alone may not be a strong determinant of audience ratings. Still, movies in the Low budget category have a wider range of ratings, with more extreme outliers on both the lower and upper ends, while High-budget films show slightly higher consistency and fewer low outliers. This indicates that while higher budgets may lead to more consistent quality, exceptional low-budget movies can achieve high ratings.

Figure 5: Average budget vs Genre (Interactive Plot) -> Slide the bar to go year by year

This bar chart displays the average budgets for films by genre from 2003 to 2024. This trend highlights the varying financial demands across genres and the heavy investment required for high-concept and visually immersive films.

How to use this graph:

  • Compare Genre Budgets: Quickly identify which genres demand higher investments versus those produced on smaller budgets
  • Analyze Trends Over Time: use the year filter to explore how the average budgets for genres have evolved over the years
